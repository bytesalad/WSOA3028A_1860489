The dot-com bubble refers to a period between the late 1990s and early 2000s during which there was a rapid rise and fall of internet commerce websites. It had consequences for many years on the global economy. There existed a strong belief that if people invested more money into new tech companies sprouting out on the internet, they would grow into massive enterprises with favourable returns. It would not be untrue to say that there was indeed growth.

Biography

Johnathan Mbeki was a Jazz bassist/composer living in Sophiatown, Johannesburg, South Africa during the 1950s. He was the bandleader and lead composer for Mbeki's Swing; an Afro-Jazz and Gospel band.

He was the only child of Annie and Simon Mbeki. For most of his childhood, he lived in a single-parent home with his mother Annie. Due, unfortunately to lung cancer, his father passed away when Johnathan was only 5 years old. Johnathan was commonly referred to by his peers and members of the community as "Papa Johnny" because of his mature and wise outlook on life especially in his younger days.
